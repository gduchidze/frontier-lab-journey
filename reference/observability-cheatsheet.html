Observability Cheat Sheet

PromQL patterns, serving-metric definitions, and the queries behind every dashboard panel in this course.

The 3 PromQL patterns (cover ~90% of use)

# 1. GAUGE — read as-is, aggregate across pods
sum(vllm:num_requests_running)
vllm:kv_cache_usage_perc                       # per-engine, labeled by pod

# 2. COUNTER — always wrap in rate() over a window
rate(vllm:generation_tokens_total[1m])         # output tokens/sec
sum(rate(vllm:request_success_total[5m]))      # completed req/sec

# 3. HISTOGRAM — quantile over rate of buckets
histogram_quantile(0.9,
  sum(rate(vllm:time_to_first_token_seconds_bucket[5m])) by (le))

Rules of thumb: window ≥ 2× scrape interval · by (le) is mandatory in the quantile pattern · sum(... ) by (pod) to split lines per engine.

Scrape-debugging checklist (target missing/DOWN)

  1. ServiceMonitor selector.matchLabels matches the Service's labels (not the Pod's)?
  2. endpoints.port is the Service port name (not a number)?
  3. Installed with serviceMonitorSelectorNilUsesHelmValues=false (or ServiceMonitor carries the release label)?
  4. Endpoint actually serves: kubectl exec ... -- curl -s localhost:8000/metrics | head?
  5. Pod Ready? Not-Ready pods drop out of Service endpoints → nothing to scrape.
