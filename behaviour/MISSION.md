# Mission: Ace Frontier-Lab Behavioral & Culture Rounds

## Why
The user is preparing for AI engineer roles at Anthropic, OpenAI, and DeepMind (sibling workspace: `system-design/` covers the technical pillar). Behavioral/culture rounds at these labs carry as much weight as technical rounds and are where most candidates stumble — Anthropic's 45-minute culture interview in particular rejects performed answers and tests live reasoning about values under pressure. The user needs a story bank plus genuine values-reasoning fluency, not scripts.

## Success looks like
- A bank of 8–10 bullet-outlined stories, each mapped to ≥3 competencies (ownership, conflict, failure, leadership, ambiguity, cross-functional influence)
- Can answer any reported question from the three labs' banks without repeating a story across a 4–6 round loop
- Can reason aloud about ethically gray scenarios honestly — including saying "I was wrong" and "I don't know" — rather than reciting
- Can articulate a personal ethical framework and where it was actually tested (Anthropic's core probe)
- Lab-specific literacy: Anthropic mission/values ("high-trust/low-ego", safety-first), OpenAI ownership-under-ambiguity culture, DeepMind collaborative-research culture
- Passes 2+ behavioral mock interviews with senior-engineer feedback

## Constraints
- Stories must be bullet outlines, never scripts — rehearsed delivery reads as inauthentic and is an explicit failure mode at Anthropic
- All stories must be true and specific (metrics, names of roles, real stakes); the user's real experience is the only raw material
- Short lessons; working engineer

## Out of scope
- Technical rounds (system design → `system-design/` workspace; coding → future workspace)
- Salary negotiation (separate topic, after an offer exists)
- Resume/recruiter-screen optimization
