Reported Question Bank

Accumulated from candidate reports (IGotAnOffer, Exponent, Preper). Used by the teacher for drill mode. Grows as new reports surface.

Anthropic — culture round (45 min, live values-reasoning)

  • Tell me about a time you did something that conflicted with your own values.
  • What is your personal ethical framework, and when was it actually tested?
  • How do you think about AI safety? What risks worry you in deploying models?
  • Tell me about a time you chose reliability/safety over speed. What did it cost?
  • Describe a decision you made under moral ambiguity — how did you reason through it?
  • When have you been wrong about something important? How did you find out?
  • Tell me about a time you pushed back on leadership. What happened?
  • Grading: honest skepticism > enthusiasm; demonstrated humility; reasoning quality in gray areas. Rehearsed answers fail.

OpenAI — behavioral (equal weight to technical)

  • Tell me about a project you owned end-to-end. What would have failed without you?
  • Describe navigating a project with completely unclear requirements.
  • Tell me about working with a difficult cross-functional partner.
  • When did you take on something far outside your comfort zone?
  • How do you think about AGI safety and responsible deployment? (probed seriously)
  • Tell me about a time you disagreed with a technical decision and were overruled.
  • Grading: ownership under ambiguity, cross-functional collaboration, genuine safety engagement. STAR works here.

DeepMind — culture round

  • How do you handle constructive criticism? Give a specific recent example.
  • Tell me about a research-style collaboration — how did you divide credit and work?
  • Describe adapting your approach based on feedback from peers or mentors.
  • What are your views on AI ethics and the societal impact of this work?
  • Tell me about a conflict inside a team you were on. Your role in resolving it?
  • Why DeepMind — specifically? (generic enthusiasm is the reported killer)
  • Grading: teamwork, feedback absorption, mission specificity. STAR works here.

Drill protocol lives in NOTES.md: say "drill me" + lab name, teacher fires random question, you answer aloud, critique follows against that lab's grading line.
